Important Teamwork Skills

Teamwork skills help you work well with others, which is useful for regular conversations along with more formal settings such as meetings and continued projects [1]. Some of the most important teamwork skills include communication, active listening. responsibility and honesty.

Skills that are part of Teamwork

Being able to work well with others is useful no matter what field you are in and will benefit your company [1]. This will also benefit you since making connections can help you advance your career.

When communicating, make sure to share relevant ideas and information [1]. Avoid sharing information that is irrelevant.

Responsibility involves handling assigned work in the proper and expected manner [1]. Being responsible helps a team perform optimally when working toward a goal.

Empathy is understanding how other people feel and the motivations behind their actions [1]. Demonstrating empathy, or showing that you understand how someone feels and taking action based on that, helps you work well with others.

When you are part of a team, you are not the only person on that team [1]. You need to focus on how your actions affect the team and how everyone else on the team feels. Make sure that you do not talk too much so that everyone can share their ideas. You also want to support others so that everyone can share their ideas. Sometimes though, you may not be talking enough, which is not helpful. Make sure to contribute your ideas so that you can support the team.

How to improve at Teamwork

Teamwork is a difficult skill to learn, but there are ways that you can improve at it.

First, you want to receive feedback from someone you can trust so that you can determine your strengths and weaknesses [1]. An outside view is best for determining your strengths and weaknesses. From there, you can work with that person on further developing your strengths and minimizing your weaknesses.

Second, you want to make SMART teamwork goals, which are goals that are specific, measurable, attainable, relevant and time-based [1]. You will be able to know when you are making progress towards these goals and that they will benefit you. The timed component will keep you focused on reaching your goals.

Third, practicing teamwork and tracking your progress will help you grow better at teamwork [1]. Lastly, if you see someone at work or in your life that has great teamwork skills, try to see how they are able to work well with others and mimic that.

Teamwork is a skill that will help you and will help your career.
