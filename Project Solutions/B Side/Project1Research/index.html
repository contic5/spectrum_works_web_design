Renewable Energy

What is renewable energy?

Renewable energy is energy that comes either natural resources such as the wind or sun or processes that replenish quickly.

Non-renewable energy comes from resources that takes a long time to replenish.

Importance of renewable energy

While both renewable and non-renewable resources are limited, for instance the sun only shines for a certain part of the day, non-renewable resources take much longer to regenerate. Gasoline for cars uses refined crude oil that is from the prehistoric times. The sun shines for on average 12 hours a day, meaning it takes much less time for the sun's renewable energy to regenerate than for oil's non-renewable energy.

Types of renewable energy

Types of renewable energy include

  • Solar Energy
  • Wind Energy
  • Hydro-electric Power
  • Bio-mass Energy
  • Geo-thermal Energy

Solar Energy


Solar energy absorbs the sun's rays to provide power. This is often used in solar pannels.

Wind Energy


Wind energy spins large fans that are connected to an electric generator and create elctricity. Wind power is also the cheapest energy source in many parts of the country.

Hydro-electric Power


Hydroelectric power converts moving water's force into electricity by turning underwater turbines that are connected to an electric generator.

Bio-mass Energy


Burning organic material that comes from plants or animals, crops or wood, generates heat that can be used by a steam turbine to generate electricity. Click on the above image for more info.

Geo-thermal Energy


Geothermal energy uses heat from deep underground to generate elctricity. Hot underground water is pumped through a turbine to create electricity.
