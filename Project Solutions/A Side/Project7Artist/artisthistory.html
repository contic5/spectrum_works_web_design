Artist History

Yoko Shimomura was born in 1967 in the Hy≈çgo prefecture of Japan. In 1988, she graduated from the Osaka College of Music [2]. She began her career at Capcom and composed music for games such as Final Fight and Street Fighter II. After working at Capcom, she worked for Square and wrote music for multiple popular series, including Kingdom Hearts. She later became a freelance musician and started the production company Midiplex. While at Midiplex , she continued writing music for the Kingdom Hearts Series and Final Fantasy XV as well. Many of her other famous games she worked on include Super Mario RPG, the Mario and Luigi Series and Xenoblade Chronicles [3].

Shimomura draws influence from musicians such as Beethoven, Chopin and Ravel [4]. Her music tends to emphasize the violin and piano, her primary instrument [4]. She has been a fan of video games such as the original Super Mario Bros and Takeshi's Challenge. Her favorite piece of music she composed is Dearly Beloved, the main theme of Kingdom Hearts [5].
