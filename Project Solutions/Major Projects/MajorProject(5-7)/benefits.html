Benefits

Physical Benefits [1]

Some of the main benefits Taekwondo offers include:

  • Balance- Both poomse (form) and kicking help with balance
  • Flexibility- Taekwondo involves regularly stretching
  • Stamina- Taekwondo's challenging exercises help build stamina
  • Strength- Both the upper body and lower body are focused on when training
  • Posture- Posture is focused on

Mental Benefits [1]

Some of the main mental benefits Taekwondo offers include:

  • Improved confidence- Taekwondo helps you relalize what you are capable of
  • Improved self esteem- When you work hard at something such as Taekwondo, you will believe in yourself more.
  • Improved concentration- Classes are often 45 minutes to an hour. Concentrating through an entire class helps build concentration.
  • Improved leadership skills- Students often lead and demonstrate during Taekwondo.
  • Improved self-discipline-Between staying focused and pushing through drills, Taekwondo requires self discipline. Through using self discipline in Taekwondo, you will improve at it.
  • Reduced stress- Exercise helps reduce stress.

Where to find benefits

If these benefits sound good to you, I would recommend finding a local Taekwondo school in your area. There were about 15,896 martial arts schools in the United States in 2016 [2]. There are 924 martial arts schools in New Jersey alone [3]. Attend class at one of these schools and see how you find it. Typically the first few classes are free so that you can determine if you like doing martial arts and want to stay at that school.
